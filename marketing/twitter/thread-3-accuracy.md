**Thread:**

1/ The question everyone asks: "But is it actually accurate?"

Short answer: yes, and we can prove it. Every prediction GOD makes is scored against the actual outcome. Transparent. Verifiable. No hiding.

Here's how we think about accuracy 🧵

2/ We don't just track "was the prediction right or wrong." We track calibration.

If GOD says something is "70% likely," it should happen ~70% of the time. If it says "30% likely," it should happen ~30% of the time.

That's what calibrated forecasting means.

3/ Why calibration matters more than raw accuracy:

A system that says "50% likely" for everything will be right 50% of the time. But it's useless — it tells you nothing.

A calibrated system that says "90% likely" and is right 90% of the time? That's actionable. You can make decisions on that.

4/ Every prediction GOD makes is logged. Over time, we build an accuracy profile:

- Overall calibration score
- Accuracy by category (politics, economy, crypto, etc.)
- Accuracy by confidence level
- Trend over time (is it getting better?)

5/ The best part: the system improves. As more predictions are made and scored, the ensemble adapts. Models that perform well get more weight. Models that underperform get less.

It's a self-improving oracle.
